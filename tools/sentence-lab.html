Word Classes

Noun — A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea
Verb — A word that describes an action, state, or event
Adjective — A word that describes or modifies a noun
Adverb — A word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb
Preposition — A word that shows the relationship between a noun and another part of the sentence
Determiner — A word that introduces a noun — such as the, a, this, or my
Pronoun — A word that takes the place of a noun — such as he, she, it, or they
Conjunction — A word that connects words, phrases, or clauses — such as and, but, or because

Clause Types

Main clause — The core of the sentence — it makes sense on its own
Relative clause — A clause that gives more information about a noun — often introduced by who, which, or that
Adverbial clause — A clause that tells us when, where, why, or how — often introduced by because, although, when, or if
Noun clause — A clause that acts like a noun — it can be the subject or object of the sentence
