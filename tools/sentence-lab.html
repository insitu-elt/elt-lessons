Word Classes

Noun — Names a person, place, thing, or idea
Verb — Describes an action, state, or event
Adjective — Describes or modifies a noun
Adverb — Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb
Preposition — Shows the relationship between a noun and another part of the sentence
Determiner — Introduces a noun — such as the, a, this, or my
Pronoun — Takes the place of a noun — such as he, she, or they
Conjunction — Connects words, phrases, or clauses — such as and, but, or because

Clause Types

Main clause — The core of the sentence — it makes sense on its own
Relative clause — Gives more information about a noun — often introduced by who, which, or that
Adverbial clause — Tells us when, where, why, or how — often introduced by because, although, or if
Noun clause — Acts like a noun — it can be the subject or object of the sentence
